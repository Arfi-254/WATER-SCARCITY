The Crisis

Water Challenges in
Northeastern Kenya

🌵

Severe Drought

Garissa, Wajir, and Mandera face prolonged droughts that dry up seasonal rivers and water pans, leaving millions without adequate water for months.

⚙️

Broken Infrastructure

Many boreholes and hand pumps fall into disrepair. Without a reliable reporting system, communities suffer for weeks before maintenance teams are dispatched.

📍

Remote Locations

Dispersed settlements mean communities travel 10–30 km to fetch water, losing productive hours and exposing women and children to safety risks.

💧

Water Quality

Contamination from pastoralist activities, poor sanitation, and stagnant water pans cause waterborne diseases that disproportionately affect children under five.

🗺️

Poor Data

County water offices lack real-time information on which sources are functional, making resource allocation inefficient during emergency responses.

👥

Community Gap

No formal channel exists for communities to communicate directly with water authorities, creating delays between breakdown and repair.
